Scopes and limits

Scope: By the submission date of this assignment, our group should have time to produce a digital prototype which would present our smart lockbox, with working hinges on the inside, and an NFC attachment which should show consumers a rough draft of what the box would look like, and how it would work. Along with this we should produce a draft app which would highlight an interface for what it would look like for consumers, and for delivery workers who use the box. Limits: producing an actual box within the time frame of this assignment seems unlikely, so unfortunately, we have opted for an online alternative, along with this, the app design will not be front end polished, nor fully functioning with the NFC device, while it would be ideal to have them working and examples, the time limit on this assignment realistically only leaves time for concept work (especially with delivery times on online orders for us to put this together)

    • Testing

    Before we start testing this project, our group must create a test plan. This test plan includes various outcomes and addition information of the test. The product which we will be testing is the app which comes with the product. The goal of this test is observing how users navigate through our app. This can be used to judge how those of our potential users think when using applications foreign to them. Some equipment required could be eye tracking software and recording the sessions with cameras. In these test users must be able to look for the smart box location, which was assigned to them, as they must deliver their product to the right box in the correct location. The type of testing which would be used would be a formal usability lab.

    Formal usability lab

    Our testing will involve a formal usability lab. This is a facility which is designed in a way that is:

  • Low-traffic low noise area
  • Good lighting
  • Electrical ventilation and ceiling requirements
  • One-way mirror
  • Digital audio/video screen capture
  • Event logging
  • Editing system

    • A plan of tasks will be given to each of the testers. This is in the form of a scenario.

    Metrics taken into observation will be:

  • Time taken to complete tasks, starting state, finished state, extra information required and possible deviations.

    • Risks

    There are many risks that are posed to this project idea, both business wise and functionality wise, that would affect the functionality of this project and its success in practice.

  • Business wise

    • There may not be a need for this product to be used, post companies and consumers, may in general, be fine with the way things are, and aren’t particularly worried about mail or parcel safety as they live in an area that is less likely to be affected by extreme weather, or thieves. So, it would be an opt in for consumers to make that choice, which could end up meaning not a lot of people want to try it or find it too much of a hassle. The other end of that is postage-delivery businesses may decide its easier and would take less time for them if they did not deliver to people with these secure lockboxes. If we do not make the project simple and effective, and provide them with good enough reasons as to why they might need it, they may end up not being used at all, and people would rather have their normal mailbox.

    Group and processes

  • Functionality wise

    • There are a few functions from the beginning that may pose a risk to the project's success. The design of the box may be the primary objection that users would have to opting into this product. Along with its size and having it placed somewhere on their property to be locked down may not be something that renters would be allowed to install, along with people not wanting to change the way their property might look, even if it’s a small change. The box must be completely weather proofed, it needs to be tight enough to avoid rain damage, and solid enough to avoid heat damage and prevent forced entry, and so if these measures aren’t put into place from the beginning, it would be hard for consumers to see a solid reason as to why they would want the product. The use of NFC’s also poses a risk, first if the NFC stops working, people will not be able to easily get their packages, so a backup option must be implemented. But the possibility of thieves hacking the box in some way by using their own rigged NFCs could also deter users from bothering to get the box in the first place, as thieves may look for houses with these boxes and seek them out, painting a target on the house essentially.
